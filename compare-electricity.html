Types of Business Electricity Contracts

Apart from domestic electricity tariffs, all commercial electricity or business electricity tariffs will have a minimum contract term. Let’s go through some of them.

  • Fixed-Rate Tariffs - With a fixed tariff, the unit rate and standing charge for your energy remain constant for a set period, usually 1 to 3 years. This provides price stability and protection against energy price increases during the fixed term.
  • Flexible or Variable Rate Tariffs - A variable tariff, also known as a standard tariff, does not have fixed prices. The rates can change at any time, often in response to fluctuations in the energy market. While it offers flexibility, it can also result in higher bills if prices increase.
What Affects Increase in Business Electricity Prices

If you are experiencing an increase in your business electricity bills, despite the decrease in electricity prices. This may be affected by various factors.

The overall supply and demand for electricity in the energy market can impact prices. Seasonal changes, weather conditions, and geopolitical events can all affect supply and demand. The type of contract you have with your energy supplier can significantly affect prices. Fixed-rate contracts provide price stability, while variable contracts can lead to price fluctuations.

The amount of electricity your business uses can influence your pricing. High energy consumption can often lead to lower unit prices. Your business's location can impact electricity prices as regions with more energy supply options and competition may offer more competitive pricing. Government regulations, taxes, and incentives can influence electricity prices. For example, subsidies for renewable energy can affect pricing.

Some businesses have tariffs where the time of day you use electricity affects the price. Off-peak usage is typically cheaper. The number of energy suppliers in your area can impact prices. More competition can lead to better deals for businesses.

These are some key factors that affect your certain increase in electricity bills.

How can I save additional costs on my electricity bill?

If you need to reduce your electricity costs, here are a few measures that not only save you money but also contribute to a more sustainable and eco-friendly operation. One of the most impactful changes you can make is to install smart meters. These electronic devices play an important role in tracking your daily electricity consumption accurately, eliminating the need for estimated bills. This precision in billing ensures you only pay for the energy you've used, preventing overcharges and unexpected costs.

Furthermore, there are government support programs designed specifically for small businesses to facilitate the installation of smart meters. These programs align with a broader mission to reduce energy consumption and promote environmental responsibility. By embracing this technology, you not only enhance cost control but also play your part in conserving valuable resources.

In addition to adopting smart meters, consider a holistic approach to energy efficiency. Making the switch to low-energy LED light bulbs not only reduces your electricity consumption but also results in longer-lasting bulbs, minimizing replacement costs. Implement timer switches to ensure lights and equipment are only in use when needed, thus reducing wastage. Taking the extra step to turn off electronic devices instead of leaving them on standby mode can yield energy savings over time.

These measures may seem small, but their cumulative impact is substantial. They not only trim your electricity expenses, benefitting your bottom line but also reduce your carbon footprint, contributing to a more sustainable and environmentally conscious business. By incorporating these practices, you're not only saving money but also investing in a greener and more efficient future.

How Do I Switch Business Electricity Suppliers?

You can't change your energy supplier whenever you want, or you might have to pay exit fees. But you do have a good opportunity to switch when your contract is nearly finished. About 49 days before your current contract ends, you can start looking for a new supplier without worrying about extra charges. This way, you can change suppliers smoothly and save money.

Switching your business electricity supplier for potential cost savings and improved services involves several steps. First, research and compare various energy suppliers, considering pricing, contract terms, and your business needs. Review your current contract, checking for exit fees and its end date. Request a customized quote from your chosen new supplier, then compare offers from your current and prospective suppliers to ensure financial alignment with your budget.

Once you decide, your new supplier contacts your current one to manage the switch. There's a brief cooling-off period, around 14 days, to change your mind without penalties. Provide a final meter reading if requested to prevent billing discrepancies. The switch takes about 21 days with no disruption in energy supply, and you'll receive bills from your new supplier. Regularly monitor your bills to ensure they reflect your new contract's terms and pricing.
